Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I study in China without knowing Chinese?▼

Yes! Over 2,000 programs are fully taught in English. However, learning basic Mandarin is highly recommended for daily life and future career prospects.

What are the entry requirements for Chinese universities?▼

Requirements vary by university and program. Generally, you need a completed secondary or undergraduate degree, academic transcripts, a valid passport, and a language proficiency certificate (IELTS/TOEFL for English programs or HSK for Chinese programs).

When should I start my application?▼

Most Chinese universities accept applications from October to April for the September intake. The CSC scholarship deadline is typically in March. We recommend starting the process at least 6–8 months before your desired start date.

Is a Chinese degree recognized internationally?▼

Yes. Degrees from China's top universities are recognized globally. Chinese university degrees carry high value in Asia, Africa, and increasingly in Europe and North America, particularly in STEM and medical fields.

How does Abreams help with the application process?▼

Abreams provides end-to-end support: university selection, document preparation, application submission, scholarship guidance, acceptance letter follow-up, visa application support, and pre-departure orientation.

Is China safe for international students?▼

China is consistently ranked among the safest countries for international students. University campuses are secure, well-maintained, and home to large international student communities from over 60 countries.

Can I work part-time while studying in China?▼

International students on a student visa may work part-time on-campus with permission from their university. Off-campus work requires an additional permit. Scholarship students are generally advised to focus on their studies.

About CSCA

What is the CSCA?

The CSCA (Chinese Student/Scholar Certification Assessment) is an assessment framework used by Chinese universities and scholarship bodies to evaluate the academic aptitude, language skills, and readiness of international applicants.

A strong CSCA profile significantly increases your chances of admission to top Chinese universities and access to full government scholarships. Abreams is one of the leading CSCA preparation and guidance providers for international students.

Exam Structure

The CSCA consists of the following components:

Academic Aptitude Test40%
Language Proficiency30%
Statement of Purpose20%
Recommendation Letters10%
